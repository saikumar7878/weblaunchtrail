Kick-Start

The WebLaunch team hosts an initial meeting with the client to discuss the scope of the project, including platforms and channels, goals, target audience, existing brand strategies, and more.


In this, WebLaunch strategist – who leads the project – will gather the necessary information to conduct in-depth competitor research and complete a comprehensive digital strategy.

WebLaunch Strategy

After the kick-start call, the WebLaunch strategist will utilize the brand strategy document that was completed in step 1 to build a customized digital strategy.


Each strategy is personalized, depending on the client’s goals, target audience, and the improvements that a website or app needs in order to be successful and kick start the progress.

Informaton Architecture

weblaunch strategists and information architects will work closely together to document the existing features and functionalities of the current platform.


Then, they work together to create a new and improved user experience, documenting all elements for designers and developers and ensuring they are reflected in later stages.

Design Mockups

After the website architecture is approved, designers will create wireframes – which are essentially blueprints of a website that outline the structure.


After wireframes are designed, web designers will add colors, logos, and branding elements to create a static image-like design of what the web pages will look like to the user.

Coding & Development

Then we move into the development stage. Here, front-ender's will create the frontward-facing interface – which customers will see – while the back-ender's will create the content management system and tie that administrative dashboard to the front-end.

Quality Assurance

Launch & Optimization

Once the platform is built and the testing is completed, the new website will be pushed live to the public. However, the work doesn’t stop there.
